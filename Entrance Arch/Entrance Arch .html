Entrance Arch, Wentworth Street


As one walks down Brick Lane:  off Wentworth Street, to the right:  All that remains is the entrance arch!  The buildings, erected by a group of (mainly Jewish) philanthropists and businessmen, don’t exist but many others do from similar projects.  The objective of such endeavors was to provide better housing for workers.  It’s “4%” because that’s what was promised to the investors, in addition to the charitable subscriptions.  My friend Clive refers to this as “The Rothschild Archway.”

The best guide to the Jewish East End, besides this one, of course, is that prepared by Clive Bettington for Tower Hamlets.  Get a hold of their walking-tour map and guide.
