Israel Zangwill


From York Hall you can take a longish walk down the Old Ford Road.  Again, it’s worth the trek all the way down to Victoria Park.  The point here is to recall and reflect on one of the most famous figures in all of Anglo-Jewish history, Israel Zangwill.  Zangwill moved to this part of the East End after he had become pretty successful.  It’s not dank and crowded.  Bryan Cheyette is writing a new biography of Zangwill.

Near Mendoza’s home and York Hall is one of the former residences of Israel Zangwill, on Old Ford Road.  Zangwill is remembered as a great and important writer, but I fear that most of his work in nearly unreadable now.  He was, however, a far more important political figure than most people assume.  He helped Zionism get a foothold in Britain, and was an unusually popular figure among Jews worldwide.
After the British government offered the Zionist movement a change for settlement in East Africa (called ‘Uganda’), which was not pursued, he was a leader of the ‘Territorial’ or ’Territorialist’ movement which has been much derided.  It was probably one of the more serious such efforts of all time.
In the coming year (2015-16) UCL will be acquiring a small but superb collection of Zangwill publications and other material, donated by Tony Yablon.
