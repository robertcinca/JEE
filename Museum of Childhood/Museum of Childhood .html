Museum of Childhood


Museum Gardens is just south of the V&A Museum of Childhood (formerly the Bethnal Green Museum of Childhood). 

http://www.vam.ac.uk/moc/

The museum is now part of the Victoria & Albert, one of the world’s greatest collections of applied arts and crafts.   Why mention it here?

Image: Jews and toys!
Some of the toys that became seen as quintessentially British were created or developed by Jewish immigrants.  The ‘Matchbox’ brand of toy cars derived from the “Mekko” company, whose principal figure was Moses Kohnstam. You’ll find these at the Bethnal Green Museum of Childhood.

Again, from the Bethnal Green Museum of Childhood:  My favorites are “Corgi” cars (nothing to do with the dogs so beloved by the royal family.)  There are several toys in the museum from Tipp & Co., which was founded by the Ullman family in Germany.  They were forced to sell their company at a much distressed price in Nazi times.  Members of the family, though, made their way to England and established the “Mettoy.”  Corporation.  This is the company that produced “Corgi.”
