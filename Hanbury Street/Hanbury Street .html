Hanbury Street
Chinese food


If you’re looking for a good Chinese restaurant in the East End, try Sichuan-Folk.  There’s a much greater range of dishes than your average Chinese restaurant, and they dare to spice things up.  It’s on Hanbury Street, E1 6QR, just off of Brick Lane.  Try the frog’s legs.
(Glatt trayf.*)  Make sure to spit out the bones.
*glatt trayf—not just unkosher—really, really unkosher


Hanbury Street:  Home of Bud Flanagan (1896-1968):  nice Jewish boy?
Born Chaim Reuben Weintrop (possibly a corruption of "Weintraub"), he took the name from his drill sergeant he didn’t particularly care for.
As a soldier in World War I he was gassed (and temporarily blinded) at Vandelle Wood.
He didn’t turn out like that more famous guy who got wounded, gassed, and temporarily blinded—a certain Adolf Hitler.  But he managed to make fun of Hitler in the film “Gasbags” (1939).
His father ran a barber shop and sold tobacco products.
“Bud” had varied and fabulous career on stage (of almost all types) and screen, leader of the "Crazy Gang"—a team three duos and one hanger-on.
He remained a pretty jewy-Jew, even in his various roles as a performer, thoughout his career.
He was seriously talented and an excellent singer.
Many of the Crazy Gang movies are available on youtube.  The most fascinating of them is "Gasbags" (1941), but others are worth viewing, such as "A Fire Has Been Arranged" (1935).
