Other Areas of Jewish Interest


Areas outside of the East End, and not currently sites of concentrated Jewish settlement, have distinct aspects of Jewish cultural interest, such as:

  • From Highgate to Kentish Town:  Karl Marx’s grave on the less fashionable Eastern side of Highgate Cemetery is well known. 
  • http://highgatecemetery.org
  • But where Karl Marx actually lived is rarely mentioned.  The pub the mainly frequented was the Southampton Arms.

http://www.thesouthamptonarms.co.uk

  • He picknicked at Hampstead Heath with his family.

  • In Dartmouth Park, one can see the home of the late Chimen Abramsky, one of the leading figures of academic Jewish Studies in the UK, who earlier had been an anarchist.

  • In the days of Chaim Weizmann the Zionist organization headquarters was 77 Great Russell Street.
