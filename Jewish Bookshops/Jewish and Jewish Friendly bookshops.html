Jewish and Jewish-friendly bookshops


(in America one would say “bookstores”)
(not ‘religious’ stuff, per se)

Joseph’s Bookstore


http://www.josephsbookstore.com
Joseph’s bookshop in Temple Fortune.  This is an absolutely excellent shop.  I don't think it has a NYC equal. 
Visiting there is a good excuse to try Hot Salt Beef Bar No. 2.
As long as you’re in the area, also see John Trotter in the Sternberg Centre, Finchley Central:  Manor House Books.  This is not to be confused with "Manor House" in N4.

http://www.manorhousebooks.co.uk
While you’re there, if this is your thing, you can see the Margaret Thatcher plaque from the inauguration of the site. 

Charing Cross Road Bookstores


Some other bookstores in Charing Cross Road and further out also pay special attention to things Jewish.
Sadly, it no longer exists but you can visit the site of Marks’s bookshop, 84 Charing Cross Road, inspiration for the shmaltzy book and movie. 
The son of the owner was Leo Marks, a fascinating writer himself.  He wrote the screenplay of the controversial film Peeping Tom (1960), and we can do a whole tour of sites from that film.

Owl Bookshop


For no special reason, the Owl Bookshop in Kentish Town has sponsored outstanding writers and events of Jewish interest.  Their bash for Charlotte Mendelsohn was tremendous.

This is now my local.  Small but excellent.
