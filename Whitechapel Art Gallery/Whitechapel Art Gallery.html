Whitechapel Art Gallery


Don’t get confused.  Brick Lane changes its name when you approach Whitechapel High Street.  Keep going onto Osborn Street.  Then turn right and you’ll see the fabulous Whitechapel Art Gallery. 
http://www.whitechapelgallery.org
I think it’s one of the most beautiful building reconstructions in all of London.  It’s almost more like something you’d see in Budapest or Vienna.  Look at it from across the street.


See the blue plaque for Isaac Rosenberg (between the entrance to the Aldgate East Tube station and the Whitechapel Gallery)--especially significant in recalling the First World War. Rosenberg was an exceptionally talented poet and painter.
Whitechapel Art Gallery:  the gem of the East End. Also be sure to visit the bookshop, (Walther) Koenig’s.  The original is on Charing Cross Road.   Koenig took over what had been Zwemmer’s.  http://www.buchhandlung-walther-koenig.de/koenig2/index.php?mode=lang&lang=en (Yes, it’s in German).

He says, 'Well, you said that if there was anything I wanted.' Gabriel says: 'I’mreally sorry. I can't do that.' Couldn't we just stop him and say hello?'

Stanley Kubrick-iana
For those interested in the great director, see the joke in the Whitechapel Art Gallery.
There’s also a tremendous archive of his working material at the University of the Arts in Elephant and Castle.
Yarnton Manor, near Oxford, which used to be the home of the Oxford Centre for Hebrew & Jewish Studies, was used to film one of Kubrick’s least successful films, Barry Lyndon.
